Air Mattress questions answered

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

When shopping for an air mattress, focus on thickness (at least 18 inches for comfort), built-in pump convenience, and materials like puncture-resistant PVC or flocked tops. Most quality models cost a few hundred dollars and can last for years with proper care and storage.

How thick should an air mattress be?

A standard single-high air mattress is about 9 inches tall, but a double-high mattress, typically 18 to 22 inches, offers much more comfort and better insulation from a cold floor. The extra height also makes it easier to get in and out of bed, and it feels more like a traditional mattress. For most buyers, a double-high design is worth the extra cost and storage space. If you’re using the mattress on a camping trip or in a tight spot, a single-high may work fine, but for home guests or extended use, go taller.

Built-in pump vs. external pump

A built-in electric pump is the most convenient option, just plug it in, flip a switch, and your mattress inflates in a couple of minutes. The downsides are that the pump can be noisy, and if it fails, you can’t replace it easily. Some models also use the same pump for deflation, which is a nice touch. External pumps (sold separately or included) can be faster and are often quieter, but you have to store them separately and remember to bring them. Rechargeable battery-operated pumps are a good middle ground for camping. Whichever route you choose, test the pump before your first night, nothing ruins a visit like a slow leak or a pump that won’t start.

Can you sleep on an air mattress every night?

Air mattresses are designed for occasional use, guest rooms, camping, or temporary setups. Using one every night can lead to comfort issues because the air pressure naturally drops overnight, and the mattress won’t provide the consistent spinal support of a foam or spring mattress. If you absolutely have to use an air mattress long-term, invest in a double-high model with a sturdy coil construction and a thick flocked top, and top it with a quality mattress topper. Even then, expect to top off the air every couple of nights. For everyday sleeping, a proper bed is almost always a better investment.

What size air mattress is right for me?

Twin-size air mattresses are ideal for one child or a single adult in a tight space. Full and queen sizes are the most popular for guest rooms and couples, queen offers enough room for two adults but check your tent or room dimensions before buying. King-size air mattresses are rare and can be hard to fit, but they’re great for a master bedroom alternative. Also consider the height: a tall double-high queen can feel like a real bed but weighs more and takes more storage space. Measure your available floor area and clearance before purchasing.

How do I keep my air mattress from leaking?

The most common cause of leaks is over-inflation, which stresses the seams. Inflate the mattress until it feels firm but still has a little give, you should be able to push down a few inches without fighting. Use a mattress pad or blanket underneath to protect against floor debris and sharp objects. Check the valve regularly: a loose or poorly seated plug can mimic a leak. If you do find a puncture, most quality mattresses come with a patch kit. Storing the mattress loosely rolled (not tightly folded) and in a cool, dry place extends its life significantly.

What’s the best material for an air mattress?

Most air mattresses are made from PVC or TPU (thermoplastic polyurethane). PVC is common, durable, and relatively affordable, but it can be heavy and has a slight plastic smell at first. TPU is lighter, more flexible, and often quieter, but it tends to cost more. Both can be puncture-resistant when constructed with multiple layers. The top surface is usually “flocked” with a soft fabric-like coating that feels warmer and less sticky than bare vinyl. A thicker flocking also helps keep sheets in place. For camping, look for a mattress with reinforced sides and a thicker bottom layer to withstand rough ground.

Frequently asked questions

How long should an air mattress last?

With proper care, avoiding over-inflation, storing in a cool place, and using a protective layer underneath, a quality air mattress can last three to five years of occasional use. Cheaper models may develop leaks much sooner.

Can I use an air mattress on carpet?

Yes, carpet provides a bit of insulation and cushioning, but you should still place a protective layer (like a blanket or tarp) underneath to prevent wear from carpet fibers and dirt. Avoid shag or deep-pile carpets because they can trap heat and make the mattress unstable.

Is it bad to sleep on an air mattress every night?

It’s not ideal for your sleep quality or back health. Air mattresses lose firmness during the night and don’t offer the same support as a standard mattress. Use one nightly only as a short-term solution, and consider a foam topper for better comfort.

How do I inflate an air mattress without a pump?

You can try a hairdryer on the cool setting (heat can damage the valve) or even a vacuum cleaner in reverse mode, but both are slow and awkward. Inflating by mouth is exceptionally difficult and not recommended. A small manual or rechargeable pump is inexpensive and worth having.

Are air mattresses good for back pain?

Some people find a firm air mattress comfortable for short-term back pain relief, but they rarely match the consistent support of a quality foam or hybrid mattress. If you have chronic back issues, a good topper can help, but an air mattress is not a suitable long-term solution.

What’s the difference between a single-high and double-high air mattress?

Single-high mattresses are about 9 inches tall and are more portable but less comfortable. Double-high models are 18 to 22 inches tall, feel much more like a real bed, and provide better insulation from the floor. Double-high is the better choice for guest rooms and regular use.

In shortThe best air mattress for you depends on how often you’ll use it, where, and your budget. Prioritize thickness, a built-in pump, and durable materials for a comfortable night’s sleep. With careful setup and storage, a quality air mattress can serve you well for years.
